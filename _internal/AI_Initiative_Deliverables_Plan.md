## Strategic Frame

The 60-day pilot runs April 6 – June 5 across 9 staff in three units. Four phases. Four questions to answer by June 5. The deliverables in this plan serve one purpose: make the initiative land as something the team owns and drives.

Every deliverable follows three design rules:

1. **Visual before verbal.** The information architecture leads with what you see. Dense text is a failure state.
2. **Participation is the pedagogy.** The 4Ds are learned by doing, not watching slides. Every session produces something.
3. **Distributed ownership from day one.** The facilitator role rotates. By Phase 3, staff are running sessions themselves.

## Deliverable 1: Kickoff Deck

**File:** `kickoff_deck.html`
**When:** April 6, Day 1 — ~35–40 minutes
**Purpose:** Orient the cohort, establish the frameworks, get people talking.
**Design:** DC CAP brand palette. Swiss Modern base from frontend-slides, customized. Clean grid, strong typography, generous whitespace. One idea per slide. No walls of text.

### Slide Architecture (13 slides)

**Opening Block (Slides 1–3) — 5 min**

- **Slide 1 — Title:** "We Built Something. Now We Use It." DC CAP wordmark. Pilot dates (April 6 – June 5). Cohort size (9 staff, 3 units). Minimal. Confident.
- **Slide 2 — The Frame:** "Over the next 60 days, you are co-investigators." You log what you observe, surface what breaks, teach what you learn. One sentence. Full-bleed typography.
- **Slide 3 — What's Already Built:** Visual inventory — 24 skills, governance framework, user guide, 24 seats, $600K KPMG grant. This is infrastructure waiting to be used. Icon-based layout, no paragraph text.

**Context Block (Slides 4–5) — 5 min**

- **Slide 4 — The Why:** AI fluency is a career asset. DC CAP's scholars will graduate into a world where this is expected. The people who serve them should model it. Two-column visual: what fluent users can do vs. what they get asked to do.
- **Slide 5 — How Claude Works:** One visual — the inference loop stripped to three concepts: training data + your context = output. The key insight: "Claude knows what you give it. Give it more, get more." No jargon.

**The 4Ds Block (Slides 6–8) — 12 min — this is the center of gravity**

- **Slide 6 — The 4Ds Overview:** Large animated quadrant reveal. Each D appears one at a time with its driving question. Delegation: "What should I ask Claude to do?" Description: "How do I tell it what I need?" Discernment: "Is this output any good?" Diligence: "Who owns what gets sent?" This visual becomes the recurring motif across all five deliverables. It needs to be memorable and instantly recognizable.
- **Slide 7 — Delegation + Description:** Split-screen. Left: Delegation as a decision tree (Is this task repetitive? Does it require live judgment? Can AI draft?). Right: Description as a before/after prompt comparison — same task, weak prompt vs. strong prompt using the Context-Task-Content-Constraints template. One concrete DC CAP example each.
- **Slide 8 — Discernment + Diligence:** Split-screen. Left: Discernment as a three-item pre-send checklist (Is the content accurate? Does the tone match the audience? Would you sign your name to this?). Right: Diligence as three behaviors — classify before you upload, review before you send, disclose when relevant. Anchoring line: "Claude drafted it. You own it."

**The 3 Modalities Block (Slides 9–10) — 8 min**

- **Slide 9 — Automation / Augmentation / Agency:** Three-column visual with DC CAP examples in each. Automation: "Draft this email." Augmentation: "Let's develop this grant narrative together." Agency: "Build a Project that any staff member can use for consistent outputs." Visual anchor: you are always the driver, the mode determines how much of the driving you share.
- **Slide 10 — Which Mode Are You In? (Live Activity):** Three scenarios appear on screen, one at a time. The group calls out the modality. Preston confirms and connects each answer back to the 4Ds. Gets every person talking within the first 20 minutes.
  - "You ask Claude to summarize a 30-page PDF into three bullet points." → Automation
  - "You and Claude spend 20 minutes refining the needs statement for the Gates renewal." → Augmentation
  - "You write Project Instructions so any team member who opens the Grants Project gets consistent, on-brand outputs." → Agency

**Governance + Timeline Block (Slides 11–13) — 10 min**

- **Slide 11 — The Data Classification Pyramid:** Four tiers, visual pyramid. Tier 1 (Restricted) at the top in red. Tier 4 (Public) at the base in green. Gold accent for the Tier 2 boundary line. One rule: "When in doubt, one tier more restricted than you think." This is the only governance slide in the kickoff — the full walkthrough happens in the orientation sessions (Days 3–4).
- **Slide 12 — The 60 Days:** Timeline visual — four phases, color-coded. Key milestones called out. The org closure week (April 13–19) marked. June 5 evaluation anchored. One sentence per phase: what makes it distinct.
- **Slide 13 — What Happens Next:** Three concrete things: (1) Account provisioning starts today, (2) Orientation sessions April 8–9 where you'll get hands-on, (3) Your first supervised Claude session April 20–22 with a real work task. Closes with: "This is yours. Own it."

**Moved to Phase 1 Activity Guide (previously Slides 14, 16, 18):**
- Skills Library walkthrough → becomes hands-on Skill Trigger Challenge activity
- Collaboration Workflow → introduced during supervised sessions when participants save their first output
- Commitment ritual → cut

## Deliverable 2: Phase 1 Activity Guide

**File:** `phase1_activity_guide.html`
**When:** Embedded in the published pilot calendar milestones (April 7–22, excluding closure)
**Purpose:** Structured first-use experiences that build 4D fluency before independent work begins. Each activity takes 20–30 minutes and maps directly to an existing pilot milestone.

### Activity Set (4 Activities)

**Activity 1: The Governance Walk**
*Embedded in: Orientation sessions (April 8–9)*
*4D Focus: Diligence | Modality: Automation*

Each pair receives 10 data scenarios (e.g., "A spreadsheet with scholar names and GPAs," "A budget summary with line items but no names," "Our published annual report"). Classify each using the four-tier framework without looking at the governance doc. Then check answers against the framework. Debrief: where did people disagree, and why?

*Who leads:* Preston facilitates. This activity becomes team-led in Phase 3 when training new cohorts.
*Output:* Classification sheets become evidence for the Phase 3 governance framework review.

**Activity 2: The Prompt Lab**
*Embedded in: Governance acknowledgment block (April 10–12)*
*4D Focus: Description | Modality: Augmentation*

Each pair selects a real work task from their current to-do list. Write a weak prompt (under 20 words, no context). Run it. Write a strong prompt using the four-part template (Context, Task, Content, Constraints). Run it. Compare outputs and document what changed.

*Who leads:* Pairs are self-directed. Preston reviews logs by end of April 12.
*Output:* Logged comparison entry in the Phase 1 use case tracker. Becomes the team's prompt quality benchmark.

**Activity 3: The Skill Trigger Challenge**
*Embedded in: First supervised use sessions (April 20–21)*
*4D Focus: Delegation | Modality: Automation + Augmentation*

Each participant attempts to trigger DC CAP skills using natural language — without looking at trigger descriptions. Note which triggers worked immediately, which required iteration, and which they couldn't crack. Group debrief at end of April 21: what made some triggers obvious and others opaque?

*Who leads:* Self-directed, then group debrief. Results shared in a team Slack thread.
*Output:* Crowd-sourced trigger phrase library. Preston uses this to refine skill descriptions before Phase 2.

**Activity 4: The Output Audit**
*Embedded in: Baseline capture sessions (April 22)*
*4D Focus: Discernment | Modality: Automation*

Each participant takes two Claude outputs generated during Activities 2–3 and runs them through a structured audit checklist: Is the factual content accurate? Does the tone match the audience? Is the data framing correct? Would you send this as-is? What would you change? Pairs cross-audit each other's work.

*Who leads:* Pairs, then group debrief facilitated by a rotating team member (first non-Preston facilitation of the pilot).
*Output:* Annotated audit sheets. Three patterns surfaced per pair. Feeds the Phase 2 check-in agenda.

### "What Sticks" Recall Check

Starting Week 3 and continuing at every weekly check-in, Preston opens with a 60-second recall: "Name the 4Ds. Which modality were you in most this week?" If participants can't answer fluently by Week 4, the kickoff framing needs reinforcement and the activity cards recalibrate.

## Deliverable 3: Activity Card Set

**Cards 1–2:** `activity_cards.html` — HTML, same design system as the kickoff deck
**Cards 3–6:** Monday Docs during pilot → upgraded to HTML before Q3 rollout
**When:** Weeks 3–8 (April 23 – May 27), one per week
**Purpose:** Structured activities that advance from skill practice to team teaching. Each card follows a consistent layout: competency tag, modality tag, time estimate, materials, steps, output, facilitator notes.

### Card Inventory

| Card | Title | Phase | 4D Focus | Modality | Facilitator |
|------|-------|-------|----------|----------|-------------|
| 1 | **Show Your Work** | 2 | Discernment | Augmentation | Team member (rotating) |
| 2 | **The Delegation Map** | 2 | Delegation | All three | Team member |
| 3 | **Skill Stress Test** | 2 | Discernment + Diligence | Automation | Two-person team |
| 4 | **The Briefing Challenge** | 3 | Description | Agency | Team member |
| 5 | **Use Case Showcase** | 3 | All 4Ds | Augmentation | Team member presents own work |
| 6 | **Train the Room** | 3 | All 4Ds | Agency | T3 candidates |

**Card 1 — Show Your Work:** Each person brings one Claude output from the previous week. Walk the group through prompt, response, edits, and final version. 10 minutes per person, 3 people per session. Makes work visible and normalizes iteration.

**Card 2 — The Delegation Map:** Each functional area maps their top 10 recurring tasks on a 2x2: AI-ready vs. human-required on one axis, high-frequency vs. low-frequency on the other. The quadrant with high-frequency, AI-ready tasks is the immediate deployment target. Output: a unit-specific AI use case priority list.

**Card 3 — Skill Stress Test:** Pairs attempt to break a skill — ambiguous inputs, off-topic tasks, boundary-testing requests. Document where it produces poor outputs. Structured QA. Output: bug report for the Innovation Hub with specific failure modes and suggested improvements.

**Card 4 — The Briefing Challenge:** Each person writes Project Instructions for a new Claude Project — as if briefing a capable new hire. Share with a partner outside that functional area. The partner asks: "What would confuse a smart outsider?" Strengthens Description at the Agency level.

**Card 5 — Use Case Showcase:** Structured show-and-tell. Each person presents one use case: the task, prompt strategy, output, and what they'd do differently. 5 minutes each, open Q&A. Facilitator captures notes for the governance framework review.

**Card 6 — Train the Room:** T3 candidates each deliver a 15-minute mini-training on one skill or workflow — as if onboarding a colleague in Q3. The cohort role-plays a skeptical new user. Structured feedback after each delivery: what landed, what was unclear, what question wasn't answered.

## Deliverable 4: Train-the-Trainer Toolkit

**File:** `train_the_trainer_deck.html`
**When:** Phase 3 — May 13–20 (build May 1–9)
**Purpose:** Give 2–3 T3 candidates everything they need to onboard the full organization in Q3.
**Format:** Slide deck with facilitator guide — presenter notes on second screen with anticipated questions, time cues, and activity instructions.

### Contents

- **Facilitator Orientation (5 slides):** How to run a kickoff. What questions to expect. How to handle "I don't see why I need this." How to create safety for people worried about job implications.
- **The 4Ds in Your Own Words (activity):** Each trainer explains one D without using the word itself. Forces genuine internalization.
- **Skill Demo Script:** Step-by-step live demonstration for DC CAP skills. What to type, what Claude should produce, what to highlight.
- **Governance Q&A Bank:** 15 common questions with approved answers. Including: "Can Claude see my emails?", "What if I upload something I shouldn't?", "Does this replace any jobs?"
- **Feedback Collection Protocol:** How to gather, log, and surface feedback from new cohorts.

## Deliverable 5: Capstone Showcase Template

**File:** `capstone_template.html`
**When:** Phase 4 — May 28 – June 5 (build May 15–21)
**Purpose:** Each participant presents a 5-minute capstone. Public proof of the pilot's value and handoff artifact for the June board briefing.

### Template Structure (5 slides per participant)

- **Slide 1 — My Pilot in a Sentence:** One sentence. Written by them.
- **Slide 2 — The Use Case:** One task. Before Claude, after Claude. Time saved or quality gained. Specific.
- **Slide 3 — My 4D Assessment:** Honest self-assessment. Which D was hardest. What changed.
- **Slide 4 — What I'm Teaching:** One workflow, skill, or prompt pattern they commit to sharing in Q3.
- **Slide 5 — What We Should Build Next:** One gap in current tooling. Feeds the Q3 roadmap.

## Visual Design Specifications

All five deliverables share a unified design language.

**Color Palette (from DC CAP Brand Guide — exact hex):**
- Primary: DC CAP Navy `#101A4C` (Confidence)
- Accent: DC CAP Purple `#8352FF` (Ambition)
- Supporting: DC CAP Blue `#87B7D5` (Excellence)
- Warm Accent: DC CAP Gold `#FCB017` (Legacy — approved for internal materials, Diligence competency, Tier 2 boundary highlights)
- Background: Tan `#FFF4ED` (Openness) or White `#FFFFFF`
- Dark slides: Navy `#101A4C`
- Text: Navy `#101A4C` (on light), White `#FFFFFF` (on dark)

**4D Competency Color Coding (recurring across all deliverables):**
- Delegation: Navy `#101A4C`
- Description: Purple `#8352FF`
- Discernment: Blue `#87B7D5`
- Diligence: Gold `#FCB017`

**Typography:**
- Headers: Inter Bold (or system sans-serif stack with bold weight)
- Body: Inter Regular
- Code/prompts: JetBrains Mono or Fira Code

**Slide Theme:** Swiss Modern preset from frontend-slides as base, customized to DC CAP palette. Clean grid. No decorative elements. Strong use of whitespace. Every slide communicates one idea.

**Interactive Elements (Kickoff Deck):**
- Animated 4Ds quadrant reveal (one D at a time, color-coded)
- Live activity: modality identification (Slide 10)
- Progress bar visible on all slides

**Activity Cards:**
- Tabbed navigation (HTML cards) or structured headers (Monday Docs)
- Color-coded header per 4D competency
- Print-ready layout (single page per card when printed)
- Clickable links to relevant Monday board items

**The 4Ds quadrant is the visual anchor of the entire initiative.** It appears in the kickoff deck, on every activity card header, in the recall check, in the T3 toolkit, and in the capstone self-assessment. By June 5, the team should be able to draw it from memory.
