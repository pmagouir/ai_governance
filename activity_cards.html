What This Activity Builds

Discernment grows when you see how others evaluate Claude's output. This activity makes the edit layer visible: what the model produced, what the human changed, and why. Three presenters per session, 10 minutes each. The group learns to spot patterns in where Claude excels and where human judgment closes the gap.

M Materials

✓ One Claude output from the past week (email, draft, analysis, or data pull)
✓ The original prompt that generated it
✓ The final version you sent or used
✓ Notes on what you changed and why

S Steps

1
Share the prompt
Read your original prompt aloud. The group hears exactly what you asked Claude to do. No cleanup, no polishing afterward.
~1 min
2
Show the raw output
Display Claude's response. Let the group read it for 30 seconds before commenting. First impressions matter here.
~2 min
3
Walk through your edits
Show the final version alongside the raw output. Call out every change you made: additions, deletions, rewordings, structural moves. Name the reason for each edit.
~3 min
4
Group discussion
The group asks two questions: "What would you prompt differently next time?" and "What edit could have been avoided with a better prompt?" This is where Description and Discernment connect.
~4 min

E Example Walkthrough

Prompt
"Draft a 150-word email to counselors about the April 27 check-in. Tone: collegial, advance-notice. Include: date, purpose, what they should have ready."
Raw Output
Claude produced a clean 140-word email. Correct date, right structure. But it opened with "Dear Valued Partner" and used "please don't hesitate to reach out" in the close.
Edits Made
Changed opening to the counselor's name. Replaced the canned close with a specific contact and next step. Added one sentence about DCTAG documentation status that Claude couldn't know.
Lesson
The model handled structure and tone. The human added relationship specificity and real-time program knowledge. This is the Discernment layer: knowing what Claude can approximate and what only you can provide.

R Peer Feedback Prompts

Prompt Quality
Was the prompt specific enough? What would you have added?
Edit Justification
Did the edits improve accuracy, tone, or completeness? Were any unnecessary?
Reusability
Could this prompt pattern work for similar tasks across the team?

Facilitator Notes

1
Select three presenters before the session. Aim for different functional areas each week so the group sees diverse use cases across units.
2
Protect the time structure. 10 minutes per presenter means hard cutoffs. Use a visible timer. The constraint forces focus.
3
If a presenter says "I didn't change anything," that's a finding worth exploring. Either the prompt was exceptional or the review was too light. Ask the group which they think it was.
4
Capture one reusable prompt pattern per session. By the end of Phase 2, the team has a library of proven patterns from their own real work.
Output: Documented prompt-output-edit sequences. One reusable prompt pattern per session. Builds the team's shared prompt library by May 6.

What This Activity Builds

Delegation is the strategic decision about which tasks to hand to Claude and which to keep. This activity makes that decision visible by mapping your unit's recurring work onto a 2x2: AI-readiness on one axis, frequency on the other. The quadrant with high-frequency, AI-ready tasks is your immediate deployment target. The quadrant with low-frequency, human-required tasks clarifies where your judgment is irreplaceable.

M Materials

✓ List of your unit's 10 most recurring tasks (bring prepared)
✓ This interactive 2x2 grid (below) or a whiteboard equivalent

S Steps

1
List your 10 tasks
Write down the 10 tasks that consume the most time in your functional area each cycle. Be specific: "Draft counselor cycle update email" rather than "send emails."
~5 min
2
Classify each task on two axes
For each task, ask: (a) How often does this happen? Weekly, monthly, per-cycle? (b) Could Claude produce a usable first draft, or does this require live human judgment throughout?
~5 min
3
Place tasks on the 2x2
Use the interactive grid below or your whiteboard. Type each task into the appropriate quadrant. Disagreements within your pair are productive: talk through them.
~8 min
4
Identify your top 3 deployment targets
From the "Deploy Now" quadrant (high frequency + AI-ready), select three tasks your unit will actively delegate to Claude in the coming week. Assign a modality to each: Automation, Augmentation, or Agency.
~5 min
5
Share and compare
Each unit presents their map. Look for patterns: are certain task types landing in the same quadrant across units? That's a signal for org-wide skill development.
~2 min per unit

A Modality Assignment (Top 3 from "Deploy Now")

For each deployment target, decide which modality fits. This connects Delegation back to the 4Ds.

Automation
"Do this for me." Claude handles the task end-to-end with a single prompt. Delegation + Diligence.
Augmentation
"Let's work on this together." Iterative back-and-forth where Claude drafts and you refine. Description + Discernment.
Agency
"Make this repeatable for the team." Build Project Instructions, skills, or templates so anyone on your unit gets consistent output. All four Ds.

Facilitator Notes

1
Remind participants to bring their 10-task list prepared. Cold-starting the list in session wastes half the time on brainstorming instead of mapping.
2
Watch for "AI can't do any of my work" framing. Redirect with: "Start with the most repetitive email you send. Could Claude draft it?" The entry point is usually a communication task.
3
The "Human Core" quadrant is as valuable as "Deploy Now." Naming what requires human judgment builds confidence. Delegation means knowing what to keep.
4
Cross-unit comparison in Step 5 often surfaces shared tasks (like "respond to parent inquiries") that signal org-wide skill development opportunities. Flag these for Preston.
Output: Unit-specific AI use case priority list with modality assignments. Three active deployment targets per unit for the coming week. Cross-unit patterns feed skill development roadmap.
