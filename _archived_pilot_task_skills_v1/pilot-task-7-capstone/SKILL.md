# Pilot Task 7: Capstone Portfolio

## Overview

**Weeks:** 7-8 (May 19 - June 5) | **Competency:** All four (Diligence, Description, Discernment, Delegation) | **Modality:** Agency

The capstone synthesizes the entire pilot into a single portfolio that serves three audiences: the participant (reflection and evidence of growth), the cohort (peer learning and shared standards), and leadership/KPMG (evidence of skill development and organizational AI readiness). This is the culmination artifact.

## Stage Detection

- **First contact or "start Task 7" or "start capstone"** → Stage 1: INTRODUCE
- **Submits capstone portfolio** → Stage 3: ASSESS
- **Submits revision** → Stage 3b: REASSESS
- **"Package this" or "I'm done"** → Stage 5: PACKAGE

## Stage 1: INTRODUCE

### Task: The Capstone Portfolio

**What you're building:** A 5-section portfolio documenting your pilot journey, your use case, your growth, what you'll teach in Q3, and what should be built next. This is your showcase for the June pilot celebration and the evidence artifact for KPMG reporting.

**Time estimate:** 2-3 hours for the initial 5-section portfolio, plus 45-60 minutes for revision. This is the largest deliverable — plan accordingly.

**Prerequisite:** Tasks 1-6 must be completed. The capstone cross-references your performance across all prior tasks and your prior task portfolios provide the evidence for several sections.

**Why this matters:** The capstone tests all four competencies simultaneously. Your use case demonstrates Description and Delegation (did you find a real workflow to transform?). Your 4D self-assessment demonstrates Discernment (can you honestly evaluate your own growth?). Your teaching module demonstrates Delegation at the strategic level (can you design a repeatable workflow?). Your Build Next proposals demonstrate Diligence (are your proposals grounded in governance-aware analysis?). Everything connects.

**The 5 Sections:**

---

**Section 1: My Pilot**
- Your name and unit
- Your pilot in one sentence — the main accomplishment or transformation you achieved

This is your headline. Make it specific. "I learned to use Claude" is not a headline. "I reduced our partner update email cycle from 6 hours to 90 minutes while improving policy accuracy" is a headline.

---

**Section 2: The Use Case (Before/After)**

Document a real work task that you transformed through AI integration during the pilot.

| Before Claude | After Claude |
|---------------|-------------|
| How you did this task before the pilot. Be specific: name the steps, the time, the pain points. | How you do this task now with Claude. Name the workflow: which skill or prompt pattern, what the human does, what Claude does. |

**Impact Metrics (required):**
- **Time Saved:** Quantify. "Reduced from X hours to Y hours" with evidence (cite specific instances).
- **Quality Gained:** What specifically improved? Name it. Accuracy? Consistency? Completeness? Provide evidence.

The strongest use cases document 2-3 specific instances where you used the new workflow, with before/after evidence for each.

---

**Section 3: My 4D Growth**

Rate yourself on each competency using the scale below. For each rating, provide a reflective statement grounded in specific evidence from your pilot tasks.

| Competency | Level | Evidence |
|------------|-------|----------|
| **Delegation** | [Select one] | How has your judgment about when and how to delegate work to AI changed? Cite specific task experiences. |
| **Description** | [Select one] | How has your prompting improved? Cite your Task 2 performance or specific prompt improvements. |
| **Discernment** | [Select one] | How has your ability to evaluate and improve AI output sharpened? Cite Tasks 4-5 experiences. |
| **Diligence** | [Select one] | How consistently do you apply governance practices? Cite Task 1 and ongoing data classification decisions. |

**Rating Scale:**

| Level | Definition |
|-------|------------|
| Still Exploring | "I'm figuring this out and need support" |
| Building Awareness | "I understand the concepts but struggle to apply them consistently" |
| Building Confidence | "I can apply this competency with occasional support" |
| Proficient | "I apply this competency independently and consistently" |
| Leading Practice | "I apply this independently and could coach a colleague" |

**Calibration check:** Your self-ratings will be cross-referenced against your actual task performance from Tasks 1-6. This ensures ratings are grounded in evidence, not just aspiration. If a rating diverges significantly from task performance, you'll have a chance to reconcile it with additional evidence.

| Task Performance Pattern | Reasonable Self-Assessment Range |
|---|---|
| Most dimensions at Exceeds Standard | Proficient – Leading Practice |
| Most dimensions at Meets Standard | Building Confidence – Proficient |
| Most dimensions at Approaching Standard | Building Awareness – Building Confidence |
| Any dimensions at Below Standard | Still Exploring – Building Awareness |

**Supplemental Evidence Field:** If your self-rating diverges significantly from task performance, you can provide additional evidence. For example: "My Task 1 performance was Approaching Standard, but since then I've correctly classified 12 data scenarios in my unit work, including 3 with small-cell thresholds." Post-task evidence of growth is legitimate — the calibration check exists to ensure ratings are grounded, not to limit them to task scores.

---

**Section 4: What I'm Teaching in Q3**

Design one workflow, skill, or prompt pattern that you will teach other DC CAP staff when the pilot expands in Q3 (July 2026).

Your teaching module must include:

1. **The workflow name** — What is it? Be specific.
2. **Step-by-step instructions** — Enough detail that a colleague who was not in the pilot could follow the steps independently. Name the skill to use, the prompt template, the inputs needed, and the expected output.
3. **Target audience** — Who benefits most? Which role(s) or unit(s)?
4. **Modality** — Automation, Augmentation, or Agency? Why?
5. **Governance pathway** — What data tier applies? What should the user check before sending the output?

**The test for quality:** Could someone execute this workflow from your document alone, without asking you questions? If yes, it's ready. If no, add the missing steps.

---

**Section 5: Build Next**

Identify 2-3 things that didn't work, gaps you discovered, or opportunities you see. For each:

1. **What didn't work or what's missing** — Name the specific problem or gap
2. **Why it matters** — What's the impact of not fixing this?
3. **The proposed solution** — Be specific. "Build a skill that does X" or "Change the governance framework to allow Y" or "Create a template for Z"

These proposals feed directly into the Q3 AI roadmap and the June board briefing. The more specific and grounded they are, the more likely they'll be acted on.

## Stage 3: ASSESS

### Pre-Assessment: Gather Prior Task Performance

Before scoring the capstone, ask the participant:

"To calibrate your 4D self-assessment, I need your performance levels from Tasks 1-6. Please share your final performance summaries (dimension levels), or if you have your task portfolios available, I can reference them directly. The performance data I need are:

- Task 1 (Governance Walk) — Diligence dimension levels
- Task 2 (Prompt Lab) — Description dimension levels
- Task 3 (Delegation Decision) — Delegation dimension levels
- Task 4 (Output Audit) — Discernment dimension levels
- Task 5 (Edit Decision Brief) — Discernment dimension levels
- Task 6 (Delegation Map) — Delegation dimension levels"

If the participant provides performance data, use them for the calibration check in Dimension 3. If they don't have them, assess the self-ratings based on the evidence provided in Section 3 rather than cross-referencing task performance.

### Rubric

**Dimension 1: Use Case Specificity**
- **Exceeds Standard** — Before/After names the exact task with quantified process details (steps, time, error rates). Both sides are specific. At least 2 documented instances of the new workflow in use.
- **Meets Standard** — Before/After describes a real task with some quantification. Evidence is based on at least 1 specific instance.
- **Approaching Standard** — Before/After is generic. One side is specific and the other is vague. No documented instances.
- **Below Standard** — No real use case. Purely hypothetical or describes something they plan to do rather than something they did.

*Assessment guidance:* The Before description is where most participants lose points. "I used to write emails manually" is not specific. "I used to spend 2-3 hours drafting the monthly partner update email, cross-referencing 3 Salesforce reports and our enrollment verification tracker, then sending it through 2 rounds of review" is specific. Check whether the Before provides enough detail that the improvement is measurable.

**Dimension 2: Impact Measurement**
- **Exceeds Standard** — Both time saved and quality gained are quantified with specific, credible evidence. Time savings cite specific instances ("reduced partner email from 3 hours to 45 minutes across the April, May, and June cycles"). Quality gains name the specific improvement ("zero policy errors in AI-assisted emails vs. 2-3 per cycle in manual drafts, verified through output audits").
- **Meets Standard** — Metrics are specific but based on a single instance or reasonable estimates clearly labeled as estimates.
- **Approaching Standard** — Metrics are vague ("saved a lot of time," "output was better") without quantification.
- **Below Standard** — No metrics, or metrics are implausible (claiming 95% time savings on a task that still requires substantial human review).

*Assessment guidance:* Credibility matters. A claim of "saved 10 hours per week" requires evidence that the original task took that long and that the new workflow genuinely reduced it. If the numbers don't add up based on the Before/After description, flag it. The participant should be able to defend their metrics with specific instances.

**Dimension 3: 4D Self-Assessment Calibration**
- **Exceeds Standard** — Each rating cites specific task evidence (task performance levels, specific experiences, concrete examples). Ratings align with actual task performance trajectory. The participant demonstrates honest self-awareness, including acknowledging areas where growth was slower than expected.
- **Meets Standard** — Most ratings cite evidence. Ratings are generally consistent with task performance. 1 rating may be slightly optimistic but not wildly miscalibrated.
- **Approaching Standard** — Ratings are provided but evidence is vague or disconnected from actual task performance. Multiple ratings appear optimistic relative to task performance.
- **Below Standard** — Ratings without evidence, or ratings are dramatically miscalibrated (multiple Leading Practice ratings with Below Standard task performance).

*Calibration check (if prior performance data available):*

Map prior task performance patterns to a reasonable self-assessment range:

| Task Performance Pattern | Reasonable Self-Assessment Range |
|---|---|
| Most dimensions at Exceeds Standard | Proficient – Leading Practice |
| Most dimensions at Meets Standard | Building Confidence – Proficient |
| Most dimensions at Approaching Standard | Building Awareness – Building Confidence |
| Any dimensions at Below Standard | Still Exploring – Building Awareness |

For each competency, compare the self-assessment against the relevant task performance:
- **Delegation:** Performance on Task 3 and Task 6 (Delegation dimensions)
- **Description:** Performance on Task 2 (Description dimensions)
- **Discernment:** Performance on Task 4 and Task 5 (Discernment dimensions)
- **Diligence:** Performance on Task 1 (Diligence dimensions)

If a self-assessment diverges significantly from task performance, flag it constructively:

"Your Discernment self-assessment is Leading Practice, but your Task 4 and Task 5 performance were mostly at Approaching Standard. A self-assessment in the Building Awareness – Building Confidence range would align more closely with your demonstrated performance. This doesn't mean you can't reach Leading Practice — it means the evidence from the pilot tasks doesn't support that level yet. You have a chance to provide supplemental evidence from work outside the assessed tasks. For example, if you've successfully applied discernment to real work scenarios since the tasks, describe those instances."

This flag is constructive. It builds honest self-assessment. Frame it as calibration, not criticism.

**Dimension 4: Teaching Module Quality**
- **Exceeds Standard** — Module names the specific workflow. Step-by-step instructions are complete enough for independent execution: names the skill to use, the prompt template (with example), the inputs needed, the expected output, and the governance check. Target audience is specific. Modality assignment is reasoned.
- **Meets Standard** — Module covers most elements. Steps are present but 1-2 may lack full specificity. A colleague could follow them with some trial and error.
- **Approaching Standard** — Module is a general description rather than a teachable workflow. Steps are high-level ("use Claude to draft the email") rather than instructional.
- **Below Standard** — No module, or module is too vague to teach from.

*Assessment guidance:* Apply the independence test: "Could a colleague in the target audience execute this workflow from the document alone, producing a useful output, without calling the participant for clarification?" If the answer is "probably not" because a step is missing or a prompt template isn't provided, the module needs more detail.

**Dimension 5: Innovation Proposal Quality**
- **Exceeds Standard** — 2-3 proposals that name specific problems experienced during the pilot (citing specific task experiences or workflow gaps), diagnose root causes, and propose solutions with enough detail to act on. Proposals demonstrate forward-thinking grounded in pilot evidence.
- **Meets Standard** — 2-3 proposals that identify real problems with reasonable solutions. Problems are named specifically even if root cause analysis is thinner.
- **Approaching Standard** — Proposals are generic ("we need more training," "the tools should be easier") without connecting to specific pilot experiences.
- **Below Standard** — No proposals, or proposals don't connect to the pilot at all.

*Assessment guidance:* The best proposals cite specific moments from the pilot. "During Task 3, I couldn't find a skill for creating Salesforce reports in a non-technical format for board members. I propose building a 'Board Data Brief' skill that takes sf-demo-data output and translates it into plain-language narratives with visualizations." That's actionable. "We need better tools" is not.

## Stage 3b: REASSESS

Re-score with delta comparison. The capstone revision typically focuses on strengthening the use case metrics, recalibrating 4D self-assessments, and adding specificity to the teaching module steps.

### Readiness Thresholds

- **Most dimensions at Meets or Exceeds:** Proceed to packaging. Note remaining refinements as optional stretch goals.
- **Multiple dimensions at Approaching:** Offer one more targeted revision cycle. Focus on the 1-2 dimensions with the largest gap. Allow the participant to revise just the weakest section rather than the full submission.
- **Any dimension at Below Standard:** Re-introduce the relevant concept before asking for another revision. Walk through one example together, then revise.

### How to Generate the Reflection Prompt and Facilitator Notes

**Reflection Prompt:** Before closing the assessment, ask the participant the task-specific reflection prompt above. Record their response. The prompt asks them to compare their self-assessments with their actual task performance across the entire pilot, identifying the largest gap and reasoning about what accounts for it. Include space for them to supply evidence from work outside the assessed tasks.

**Facilitator Notes:** After you've assessed the portfolio, synthesize two insights:
1. **Pattern:** What does this capstone reveal about this participant's growth trajectory? Did their confidence grow faster or slower than their task performance? Are there particular competencies where they're more or less aware of their development? Include specific examples of accelerated or plateaued growth across tasks.
2. **Discussion Question:** Frame one question that uses this specific capstone to teach the whole cohort. The question should illuminate something about the pilot experience or the competency growth process itself.
