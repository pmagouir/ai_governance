Our Experience

Real, Uneven Progress

We’re 25 people. In twelve months, AI changed what this organization can produce. Process improvement, product development, workflow automation, QA and verification infrastructure, and streamlined communications across every stakeholder group. That progress has been uneven across domains, and we are working to build the conditions that smooth adoption and confidence.

What We Learned

Data Governance First

AI will expose every ambiguity your organization has been living with. Data governance is the prerequisite to AI governance. If this needs an update, prioritize it.

01

Alignment

Do our teams measure the same things the same way, and who is accountable when they don't? Data ownership, metric reconciliation, cross-unit definitions, shared source of truth.

02

Communication

What is external vs. internal, and who decides? Donor-facing vs. internal data, website content, reporting categories.

03

Safety

What happens when something goes wrong? Access controls, breach response, data handling, incident escalation.

Honest Assessment

Four Open Challenges

01

Fear and Over-Control

Staff who see the governance boundary as a threat stay conservative. Leaders who see AI as a risk tighten controls past the point of usefulness. Both instincts are understandable, and both slow adoption.

02

Demonstrating Real Value

The gap between “I’ve heard this is useful” and “I just saved four hours on something I used to dread” is the gap we are still closing for some staff.

03

Uneven Adoption

Some team members are building agents. Others are still at the prompt level. Managing that spread without creating resentment or pressure is a leadership challenge we are actively working through.

04

Keeping This Off the Side

When AI competes with “real work” for people’s time, it loses. The tools change fast, and staying current requires deliberate institutional investment. Treating this as a side project guarantees it stays one.
