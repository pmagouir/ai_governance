Before you delegate

Four gates.

Run any task through these four before handing it to Claude. Yes on all four is the signal.

01 · Safety
Is it safe to hand off?
Data tier check. Restricted or Sensitive data is a stop. Redact, de-identify, or use the right workspace first.
02 · Time
Is it time-intensive for humans?
The bigger the human time cost, the more value Claude creates. Minute-long tasks usually do not justify the prompt.
03 · Cadence
Is it performed regularly?
Recurring tasks compound. A one-off rarely justifies the setup. The real gain shows up on repetition four.
04 · Fit
Is AI effective at it?
Claude is strong on drafting, summarizing, pattern matching. It is weak on novel judgment and relational calls.

A no is a pause. Ask what would have to change before you try again.

Example 01

Email.

Most of us write the kind of email where policy, tone, and boundaries all have to be right. Family escalations. Student outreach. Counselor updates. Partner follow-up.

Worked example: a family pushing back on a declined renewal.

H1 · Personal

Draft one escalation.

A parent emails pushing back on a declined renewal. You redact the family's identifiers, paste the message and the renewal policy into Claude, and ask for a draft that holds the decision and acknowledges the parent's position. You read it, fix two sentences, send.

Fifteen minutes became five. Policy was right. Tone was close to where you would have landed. Governance first, speed second.

Delegation

Automation. Claude performs. You review. Your judgment is the last word.

H2 · Team

Build a Project the team inherits.

You notice every escalation follows the same shape. Acknowledge, restate policy, offer the Tier 1 path, close firmly. You open a Claude Project, paste the escalation ladder, the Tier 1/2/3 language, and three redacted responses you already approved. Anyone on the student services team now produces a first draft the way you would.

The Project carries the policy. The person adds the context.

Delegation

Augmentation. Your judgment shapes the Project so the team inherits a pattern, not a one-off.

H3 · Enterprise

A skill library, already shipped.

The Project becomes a skill. Six now ship across DC CAP. Claude produces net-new email in the correct register for the audience, inside rules leadership wrote.

dc-cap-escalation
Family pushback, council inquiries, Tier 1/2/3 routing.
dc-cap-situational-email
Inbound student or family situational inquiries.
dc-cap-student-outreach
The 18 touchpoints of the scholarship journey.
dc-cap-counselor-outreach
The 5 touchpoints of the counselor series.
dc-cap-partner-outreach
The 7 touchpoints of the 14 university partners.
checking-communications
Policy, voice, and brand check before send.
Delegation

Agency. Claude operates inside guardrails leadership set. This H3 is not aspirational. It is running.

Example 02

Grant writing.

Every nonprofit writes grants. The development office writes more than most. The second example sits on top of a working system, not a hypothetical one.

The watchlist scan runs Monday. The CEO briefing ships Friday. Both are live.

H1 · Personal

Draft one LOI.

Earlier this quarter the development team drafted a Gates Foundation LOI. We loaded our retention data, the three strategic lanes, and the funder's stated priorities into Claude and asked for three opening paragraphs, each with a different angle. We picked one, revised the rest by hand, and sent.

Three angles in thirty minutes. Revising and landing the voice took the rest of the afternoon. The LOI went in on time.

Delegation

Automation. Claude performs. The judgment stays human.

H2 · Team

Five agents, running now.

01
Scout
Scans the funder watchlist Monday morning.
02
Fit Analyst
Scores every lead against the three strategic lanes.
03
Writer
Drafts in Preston's voice, register matched to funder type.
04
Auditor
Tears the draft apart across four adversarial lenses.
05
Director
Compiles Friday's CEO briefing for Eric.

Each agent is a skill in the library. Each pulls in its own kit as it works, preston-writing, funder-framing, dc-cap-org-intelligence. The single move became an agent chain the development team inherits.

Delegation

Augmentation, reaching toward Agency. Judgment shapes every step. The chain carries the policy.

H3 · Enterprise

Not yet. But the shape generalizes.

The agent-chain shape is not running in any other unit. Yet. It would fit a Student Scout, Fit Analyst, Response Writer, Auditor, and Director for family escalations. It would fit an Operations Scout, Compliance Analyst, and Auditor for recurring filings. That is what a capstone looks like.

Honest read: H3 has shipped on email, as a skill library any unit can call. H2 has shipped on grants, as a five-agent chain. H3 on grants, cloning the chain into other units, is the question we are asking each of you to answer in Week 8.

Delegation

Agency. Agents act inside guardrails each unit leader sets. The deliverable you ship in Week 8 is a candidate.
