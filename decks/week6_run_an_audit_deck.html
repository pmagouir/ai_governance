Q1 · Discernment

What does "good" mean
for the stakes I'm working at?

The plain answer

The bar moves with the audience.

A note to a teammate clears at one bar. A note to a funder clears at a different one. A board memo clears at a third. The same draft can be ready, almost-ready, and not-ready depending on who reads it.

People use one mental bar — their own. The result: tone calibrated for the writer rather than the reader. Claims sourced to memory rather than the source. An ask buried because the writer already knows what they want.

The move

Name the audience and the stakes before you grade it.

Adversarial-audit's intake makes this explicit: purpose, audience, stakes, deadline. Four questions, asked once, answered honestly.

Skip the intake and the audit averages — generic feedback for a generic audience. Answer the intake honestly and the audit sharpens to the one bar that matters.

The kicker. "Good" is a function of audience. Name it before you grade it.

Q2 · Discernment

What's a panel, and
why four lenses instead of one?

A panel is a set of expert lenses, each in strict character discipline. They don't see each other's notes — that's the design. Disagreement becomes data. Agreement becomes the verdict.

Editor
"Is there an argument here, or just observations?"
Fact-Checker
"Which claims survive a challenge?"
Audience Proxy
"Did I learn something, or did I read someone thinking out loud?"
Originality Critic
"Have I read this under a different byline?"

The kicker. One reviewer averages — strengths cover for weaknesses. Four lenses converge — disagreement becomes signal.

Q3 · Discernment

What is convergence,
and why is it the signal?

Convergence is the moment two or more independent lenses, running blind, flag the same line for different reasons.

Fact-Checker
"No source. No citation. The 73% number doesn't appear in any of the three sector reports I'd expect to source this. Cut or source."
Originality Critic
"Opening with a manufactured stat is the most common AI-thought-leadership template I see. The post announces itself as AI-generated before the second paragraph."
↑ Convergent flag — 2 agents, 2 reasons, 1 sentence
"Studies show 73% of nonprofit AI implementations fail in the first year."

The kicker. One lens flags a line and the writer negotiates. Two lenses flag it cold and negotiation stops. Convergence is the math. Trust it over the vibe.

Q4 · Diligence

What does this look like
on my own work?

Five steps. Three modes. The cadence is the build.

1
Intake
Purpose. Audience. Stakes. Deadline.
2
Mode
Light, Standard, or Full.
3
Panel
Matched to the artifact type.
4
Triage
Verdict + score + critical flags.
5
Decision
Safe to send · needs work · do not send yet.
Light

3 agents

Stage 1 triage only · 90 seconds.

Runs every time. Catches the obvious convergent flags. The team that skips light mode ships the obvious flags every time.

Standard

3–4 agents

Stage 1, then Stage 2 on request.

Default for anything about to leave the building. Counselor emails, partner updates, briefing paragraphs.

Full

4 agents

All three stages · literal rewrites.

Reserved for board, funder, external publication. Ten minutes per artifact. Worth it at the stakes; pointless at lower ones.

The kicker. Audit belongs in the workflow. Build it into the cadence, not as a one-time gate at the end.
