The taxonomy

Four containers.
Defined by what's reused.

One-shot

Reuses nothing

A deliberate exception.

You will not do this again. Say why in one sentence — that is the discipline that stops your team from rebuilding the same prompt every Tuesday.

Project

Reuses context

Same body of knowledge, varied asks.

A workspace loaded with the files, voice, and history that support draft, summarize, compare, extract — for weeks.

Skill

Reuses the move

Same procedure, varied inputs.

A short written procedure — when to trigger, what good looks like, what to avoid — anyone on the team can run. The artifact, not the human capability.

Automation

Reuses the trigger

Cadence or event-driven.

Runs on a Monday or in response to an inbox event. No human has to remember. A human still reviews before anything external happens.

Pick the wrong container and you rebuild the same prompt every week. Pick the right one and the team inherits the move.

Before you build

Four steps.

Run any task through these four before you open Claude. The worksheet on the hub walks you through it field by field.

01 · Describe
What is the work?
One sentence. Audience. Cadence. What good looks like. Vague descriptions produce mis-built containers.
02 · Reusable
What's reused?
Nothing, the context, the move, or the trigger. Pick the one that fits the work, not the one that feels productive.
03 · Container
Which one?
Each reusable thing maps to one container. Nothing → one-shot. Context → Project. Move → Skill. Trigger → Automation.
04 · Build
Make the artifact.
Project setup. SKILL.md. Trigger spec. Or the one sentence that says why this stays a one-shot. Bring it to Friday.

The deliberate one-shot is a real choice. Naming why something isn't reusable is its own discipline.

Example 01

Partner outreach.

Thirteen university partners. Year-round contact. Some emails you write once. Some shapes you write every cycle. Some moves the whole team should run the same way. Some checks no one should have to remember.

One job. Four containers.

One-shot · reuses nothing

Draft the one weird email.

A note to Georgetown's interim dean explaining why our timeline shifted this cycle. The dean is interim. The shift was a one-time event. You give Claude the context — voice, prior emails to Georgetown, the policy reason — and ask for a draft. You read, fix one paragraph, send.

The discipline is naming why: the variable that won't repeat is the interim dean herself. That sentence is the artifact. Without it, somebody on the team builds a "Timeline Shift Email Skill" next week and we are over-engineering.

What's reused

Nothing. The work won't repeat. Naming why is the discipline.

Project · reuses context

Stand up Partner Engagement.

A Claude Project loaded with the partner contact list, MOU terms, every email sent in the last year, the voice guide, current cycle dates. Now any partner-related ask runs against the same body of knowledge. Draft a meeting brief. Compare three partners' last responses. Pull every Howard reference since February. Surface what we said the last time we shifted a deadline.

The Project carries the knowledge. The person adds the ask. Three different shapes of work — drafting, lookup, comparison — run against one workspace.

What's reused

The context. Same body of knowledge. Many different asks. For weeks.

Governance

Reference files carry their tier with them. Anyone with Project access has access to that data. Decide who's in the Project before you load.

Skill · reuses the move

Draft a partner email in DC CAP voice.

Write the move down once. When to trigger it: any partner email, any topic. What good looks like: warm, professional, names the relationship, never asks twice. What to avoid: generic openers, formal closers that read cold, the AI tell-tale words. Now anyone on the team — student services, development, partnerships — drafts a partner email the way you would.

Different from the Project: nothing is preloaded. The person describes the partner and the topic inline each time. The procedure stays consistent across all 13 partners and every topic.

What's reused

The move. Same procedure. Varied inputs. Anyone on the team can run it.

Automation · reuses the trigger

Quiet Partner Watch.

Every Monday at 8 AM, surface the partners we have not touched in 30 days, with a one-line suggested reach-out for each. The trigger fires on its own. The reach-outs still pass through a human before they go out. No one has to remember to look.

The cadence is the artifact. Combine it with the Skill — Quiet Partner Watch produces the list, the partner-email Skill drafts each reach-out — and the team has a working pattern, not a heroic memory.

What's reused

The trigger. Cadence-driven. Human checkpoint stays.

Example 02

Grant pipeline, revisited.

Last week we walked through the five-agent grant chain — Scout, Fit Analyst, Writer, Auditor, Director — as one H2 move. This week, look at the same chain through the four-container lens.

The same pipeline. Four shapes inside it. This is what mature looks like.

The grant pipeline, decomposed

Same chain. Four shapes.

The pipeline that runs every Monday and ships a CEO briefing every Friday is not one container. It is four, working together. That is what every mature workflow looks like.

One-shot · reuses nothing
The Gates LOI angle we picked.
Three opening paragraphs were drafted. We chose one, revised by hand, sent. The two we threw out are not a Skill — they are evidence we considered alternatives.
Project · reuses context
The funder dossier workspace.
Each Pursue-tier opportunity gets a Project loaded with funder profile, prior gifts, DC CAP retention data, the strategic lanes. Drafts, briefs, comparisons all run against the same context.
Skill · reuses the move
Five Skills inside the chain.
Scout, Fit Analyst, Writer, Auditor, Director. Each is a written procedure — when to trigger, what good looks like, what to avoid — that runs against any opportunity in the pipeline.
Automation · reuses the trigger
Monday scan. Friday briefing.
Two cadences. The Monday watchlist scan fires the Scout. The Friday briefing fires the Director. Preston and the development team review and ship — but no one has to remember to start either run.

A working system is rarely one container. It is the right container picked at every layer.
