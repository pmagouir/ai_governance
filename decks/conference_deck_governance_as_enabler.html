The Tension Every Leader Feels

Two legitimate priorities
pulling in opposite directions

Governance & Security

We hold FERPA-protected student data. We hold donor financial records. One misuse could undermine years of trust with families, funders, and partners.

Innovation & Confidence

Staff need psychological safety to experiment. They need permission to try, fail, and iterate. Without confidence, adoption stalls and the investment produces nothing.

The question: how do you do both at once?
Why We Can't Wait

The Stakes Are Personal

AI fluency is becoming a core professional skill for every knowledge worker in the social sector. Our staff need it for their careers. Our students will need it for theirs. If we get this right, we build an organization that can compound its mission impact year over year.

DC CAP serves 1,290 scholars across 13 university partners. Every hour we free from manual reporting is an hour returned to direct student support. For context, 95% of enterprise AI pilots fail to produce measurable returns. The primary failure mode is adoption.

What We Did

We classified every data type
before writing a single prompt

We built a four-tier data classification system and role-specific permission guides so people knew exactly what they could do with AI on Day 1. We trained 10 leaders across 6 functional units before opening access to anyone else. Each leader builds a training module for their own team using real scenarios from their own workflows.

Staff moved from "I don't want to get this wrong" to active experimentation within the first week. Clear rules became permission to move.

Data Governance

Four-Tier Data Classification

Restricted
Prohibited from AI
Individual PII, FERPA records, SSNs, family income data
Sensitive
Role-Based Access Only
Internal financials, small-cell aggregates, embargoed partner data
Internal
Open to Licensed Users
Strategy docs, de-identified outcomes, organizational knowledge
Public
Unrestricted Use
Annual reports, published research, marketing materials

This system took two weeks to build. It answered 90% of "can I put this in AI?" questions on Day 1.

For Your Organization

Three Things We'd Share

01

Classify your data before your use cases

Permission guides that say "here's what you CAN do" build confidence faster than policies that list prohibitions.

02

Measure fluency, not logins

We built a five-construct instrument that surfaces the knowing-doing gap. It tells us who needs coaching, not just who signed in.

03

Train leaders first, then let them train their teams

By Week 8, every leader owns their unit's AI onboarding. Role-specific scenarios from their own workflows.
