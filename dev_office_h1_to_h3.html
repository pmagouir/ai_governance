The framework 02 / 08
A quick reminder

Three horizons of gain

Personal, team, organization. That's the whole idea. H1 helps one person. H2 helps a team. H3 changes how we work.

H1 — Personal

Faster drafts for the work on your plate

One person, one tool, one task. A better first pass. Time returned to the individual.
Gain: minutes / hours
H2 — Team

Patterns your team inherits

A shared workflow, shared sources, shared language. The next person does not start from scratch.
Gain: days / cycles
H3 — Enterprise

Moves that become how we operate

The pattern gets portable. Same scaffold, different domain. The system improves itself across quarters.
Gain: quarters / years
Horizon 1 — where we started 03 / 08
Four months ago

One person drafting one letter

A grant letter that used to take me two days of staring at a blank page became a one-hour working draft. Real gain. Contained gain.

Day 1
Cafritz LOI, first attempt. I pasted our last proposal into Claude and asked for a draft against the current RFP. It came back better than my cold draft. I edited. I sent a version to Eric. He liked it.
Day 14
Second letter, same pattern. Open file, paste context, ask for draft, edit. Each letter still required me, alone, holding every fact, every phrase, every funder's priorities in my own head.
Limit
What H1 could not do. Remember our numbers. Remember the rules. Catch me when I drifted. Teach the next teammate. One more letter meant one more tired evening.
The turn into H2 04 / 08
The shift from personal to team

Three files the whole team reads

We wrote down our facts, our phrases, and our mistakes. Every agent reads all three before starting any work.

canonical.md
The single source of truth for every number
800 scholars. 13 partners. 50% from Wards 7 and 8. Retention above 85%. If the number is not in canonical.md, no agent uses it.
glossary.md
The phrases that carry weight
Debt caps. Renewable up to 5 years. Wraparound coaching. FGLI. Career Pathway Intelligence Platform. Load-bearing phrases, written once, reused verbatim.
errors.md
Every mistake, caught and kept
Append-only log of every framing error, stale figure, and tier-confusion slip. The next session starts by reading this. The system gets smarter.
Why it matters
Three text files. That is the difference between what one person knows and what the whole team can use. Any of you could set this up for your own domain in a week.
Horizon 2 — team pattern 05 / 08
What replaced the blank page

Five roles. One handoff chain.

Each role is a skill. Each handoff is a gate. Nobody holds the whole thing in their head. The pipeline does.

Scout
Finds new RFPs. Labels source tiers.
→
Analyst
Scores fit. Builds dossiers. Flags risks.
→
Writer
Drafts against canonical + glossary.
→
Auditor
Runs four verification gates.
→
Director
Routes to Eric for CEO approval.

Four gates, one at each handoff

Gate 1
Every claim has a link
Scout cannot surface a funder without a citable URL.
Gate 2
Tier 5 gets re-verified
Indexer data cannot drive dossiers unless confirmed against the funder's own site.
Gate 3
Canonical before copy
Writer uses no DC CAP figure that is not in canonical.md.
Gate 4
Cross-document alignment
Auditor checks proposal, budget, partner letters, and cover for the same facts and phrases.
The system, actually working · Week 17 06 / 08
Proof this week

Two errors caught before a funder saw them

Both came from the same old failure mode: indexer data leaking into external-facing copy. The gates did what they exist to do.

Catch 01 · Gate 2
McGovern program specifics were indexer-sourced
Dossier had a name, a deadline, and a dollar figure pulled from Instrumentl. Re-verification against mcgovern.org would not confirm them. Targeted search then found the real block: the program is invitation-only. We moved from a false Pursue to a cold intro asking for a path to invitation — a truthful move, not a blind application.
Catch 02 · Human gate
deLaski intro named a relationship we do not have
The dossier suggested Eric open with "Education Design Lab grant precedent." Preston confirmed DC CAP has no connection to Education Design Lab. The phrase came from a Kindera tag on the funder profile, not from any DC CAP record. Intro redrafted without the fabricated hook.
Logged · errors.md
Both errors written into the append-only log
Rows 11 and 12 added to errors.md the same day. Next session's agents read those rows first. The same mistake costs us once. It does not cost us twice.
Horizon 3 — enterprise shape 07 / 08
From team to organization

The same setup fits other work

Grants is the first domain. The same three files plus the same five roles plus the same four checks work anywhere DC CAP needs one voice, one set of facts, and no drift between documents.

  • Counselor + parent communications Same discipline, student-facing voice
  • Board and committee decks Executive summary + canonical figures
  • Program design and pilot evaluation Research tier + verification gates
  • Partner university relationship management Shared facts, shared phrases, shared asks
  • Press, LinkedIn, public-facing writing Glossary + voice skill + audit
  • CPIP product documentation Data tier rules + canonical specs
The claim
Building this once was slow. Building the second version in a new domain is a weekend. That is what H3 looks like.
Close 08 / 08
What I want you to take away

Three asks for the next four weeks

For your own work
  • Pick one recurring task. Something you draft, review, or prospect weekly.
  • Write down three facts you always reach for. That is the start of your canonical file.
  • Keep an errors log. One row per caught mistake. Append only. Read it at session start.
For the pilot cohort
  • Capstone design in Weeks 4-5 is H2. A pattern your team can use, not a solo productivity win.
  • Capstone delivery in Week 8 is H3. Something the organization can keep using after you ship.
  • Build for what your team does next month. Not just for the thing you hand in.
One sentence
H1 is what AI does for one person today. H3 is how DC CAP operates a year from now. Moving between them is ordinary work: write the facts down, agree on the handoffs, log the mistakes, read them at the start of every session.
