# Facilitator Protocol: The Deployment Strategist

**Task:** pilot-task-4-strategize | **Window:** Weeks 6-7 (May 18-29, 2026) | **Participants:** 10 across 6 units

---

## When to Review

**During the task window:**
- [ ] Day 3: Check submission tracker — who has started? Nudge anyone who hasn't opened the task.
- [ ] Day 7: Mid-window check — review any completed initial assessments. Flag anyone stuck at Below Standard for personal outreach.
- [ ] Day 10: Final check — anyone who hasn't submitted initial draft needs direct contact.

**After automated assessment runs:**
- [ ] Spot-check 2 participants (20% sample) — compare your assessment to the skill's automated assessment. Note any disagreements for calibration.
- [ ] Review all Below Standard flags — these participants may need facilitator encouragement alongside the automated reteaching module.
- [ ] Review all Exceeds Standard — confirm the extension challenge is appropriate and the participant isn't coasting.

---

## What to Prioritize

**Read first:** The ROI reasoning on the top 2 triage selections and the Q3 rollout plan. The task mapping (Deliverable 1) is foundational but relatively straightforward. The strategic signal lives in how they chose which 2 tasks to deploy first and whether the Q3 plan is realistic. A participant who maps 6 tasks well but triages poorly hasn't demonstrated strategic thinking.

**The one thing that matters most:** Can they think strategically about deployment ORDER, not just which tasks are AI-ready?

**Common patterns to watch for:**
1. **All tasks classified as "Deploy Now" with no discrimination.** They map 6 tasks and mark 4-6 as ready for AI deployment. This suggests enthusiasm without strategic filtering. A strong submission should have at least 1-2 tasks held back with clear reasoning for why.
2. **ROI stated without math ("saves time").** The triage says a task "would save significant time" or "improve quality" without estimating hours saved, error rates reduced, or any quantitative grounding. Even rough estimates ("currently takes 3 hours, AI-assisted estimate 1 hour") show stronger strategic thinking than vague claims.
3. **Q3 plan is a wishlist, not a plan with constraints.** The rollout plan lists what they want to deploy but doesn't account for training needs, governance approval timelines, staff capacity, or phased rollout. A plan without constraints is a wish.

**What NOT to spend time on:** Whether the participant's 6-task mapping is exhaustive (it won't be), the exact accuracy of their time estimates, or whether you agree with their triage ranking. Focus on the quality of their reasoning and whether the plan is realistic, not whether it's the plan you would write.

## Caseload Reality

You have 10 participants and a full CSAO workload. Here's how to manage this in ~2 hours per task cycle:

| Activity | Time | When |
|----------|------|------|
| Submission tracker check | 10 min | Days 3, 7, 10 |
| Spot-check 2 participants | 30 min | After initial assessments |
| Below Standard outreach | 15 min/person | As flagged |
| Exceeds Standard review | 10 min/person | As flagged |
| Revision spot-check | 20 min | After revisions |
| **Total per cycle** | **~2 hours** | |

**Triage rule:** If you can only review 2 participants deeply, choose: (1) the Below Standard participant most likely to disengage, and (2) the Exceeds Standard participant who could become a peer teaching resource.

## Quick Reference

- **Automated assessment handles:** Initial scoring, feedback delivery, revision guidance, reassessment, portfolio packaging
- **You handle:** Encouragement, calibration spot-checks, Below Standard outreach, peer exchange facilitation, escalation decisions
- **Escalation trigger:** If a participant scores Below Standard on the same dimension across two consecutive tasks, schedule a 1:1 conversation.
