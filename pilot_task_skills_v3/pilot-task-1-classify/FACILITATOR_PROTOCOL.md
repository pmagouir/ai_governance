# Facilitator Protocol: The Governance Classifier

**Task:** pilot-task-1-classify | **Window:** Weeks 2-3 (April 13-24, 2026) | **Participants:** 10 across 6 units

---

## When to Review

**During the task window:**
- [ ] Day 3: Check submission tracker — who has started? Nudge anyone who hasn't opened the task.
- [ ] Day 7: Mid-window check — review any completed initial assessments. Flag anyone stuck at Below Standard for personal outreach.
- [ ] Day 10: Final check — anyone who hasn't submitted initial draft needs direct contact.

**After automated assessment runs:**
- [ ] Spot-check 2 participants (20% sample) — compare your assessment to the skill's automated assessment. Note any disagreements for calibration.
- [ ] Review all Below Standard flags — these participants may need facilitator encouragement alongside the automated reteaching module.
- [ ] Review all Exceeds Standard — confirm the extension challenge is appropriate and the participant isn't coasting.
- [ ] **Late / Missed Task:** If no submission by Day 13 (grace period): send final outreach. If still missing, apply the missed-task fallback from the master architecture.

## What to Prioritize

**Read first:** Classification accuracy on edge cases (Scenarios 3-6) and the unit-specific quick-reference card. The edge cases reveal whether the participant is reasoning through the framework or pattern-matching. The quick-reference card reveals whether they can apply the framework to their own unit's data.

**The one thing that matters most:** Can they explain WHY something is a particular tier, not just pick the right tier?

**Common patterns to watch for:**
1. **Right tier, no reasoning.** They pick "Tier 1" for FAFSA data but can't articulate that it's because individual financial records are PII. The classification is correct but the competency isn't demonstrated.
2. **"Sensitive" without naming what's sensitive.** They say data is "sensitive" as a vague label rather than identifying the specific risk factor (small cell size, PII, legal obligation, etc.).
3. **Quick-reference card is generic, not unit-specific.** The card lists general data types instead of naming the actual data their unit handles daily. A Student Success card should mention case notes, FAFSA details, and family financial information — not just "student records."

**What NOT to spend time on:** Formatting of the deliverable, exact word count on reasoning sections, whether they used bullet points vs. paragraphs. Focus on classification accuracy and reasoning quality, not presentation.

**If a participant reports the task is taking too long:**

> The task is designed for 2-2.5 hours including reading time. If a participant is past 2 hours and not yet done, the most common cause is spending too long on reasoning for each scenario. Tell them: "Focus on your strongest 4 scenarios and your quick-reference card first. You can refine the remaining 2 scenarios in revision." A partial, high-quality submission is better than an abandoned complete one.

## Caseload Reality

You have 10 participants and a full CSAO workload. Here's how to manage this in ~2 hours per task cycle:

| Activity | Time | When |
|----------|------|------|
| Submission tracker check | 10 min | Days 3, 7, 10 |
| Spot-check 2 participants | 30 min | After initial assessments |
| Below Standard outreach | 15 min/person | As flagged |
| Exceeds Standard review | 10 min/person | As flagged |
| Revision spot-check | 20 min | After revisions |
| Data entry (spreadsheet) | 5 min/participant | After assessment |
| **Total per cycle** | **~2.5-3 hours** | |

**Triage rule:** If you can only review 2 participants deeply, choose: (1) the Below Standard participant most likely to disengage, and (2) the Exceeds Standard participant who could become a peer teaching resource.

## Quick Reference

- **Automated assessment handles:** Initial scoring, feedback delivery, revision guidance, reassessment, portfolio packaging
- **You handle:** Encouragement, calibration spot-checks, Below Standard outreach, peer exchange facilitation, escalation decisions
- **Escalation trigger:** If a participant scores Below Standard on the same dimension across two consecutive tasks, schedule a 1:1 conversation.
