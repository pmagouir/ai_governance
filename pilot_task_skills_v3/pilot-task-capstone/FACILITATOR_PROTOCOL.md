# Facilitator Protocol: The Pilot Portfolio

**Task:** pilot-task-capstone | **Window:** Weeks 7-8 (June 1-12, 2026) | **Participants:** 10 across 6 units

---

## When to Review

**During the task window:**
- [ ] Day 3: Check submission tracker — who has started? Nudge anyone who hasn't opened the task.
- [ ] Day 7: Mid-window check — review any completed initial assessments. Flag anyone stuck at Below Standard for personal outreach.
- [ ] Day 10: Final check — anyone who hasn't submitted initial draft needs direct contact.

**After automated assessment runs:**
- [ ] Spot-check 2 participants (20% sample) — compare your assessment to the skill's automated assessment. Note any disagreements for calibration.
- [ ] Review all Below Standard flags — these participants may need facilitator encouragement alongside the automated reteaching module.
- [ ] Review all Exceeds Standard — confirm the extension challenge is appropriate and the participant isn't coasting.

---

## What to Prioritize

**Read first:** Section 3 (self-assessment on Diligence and Delegation) and Section 4 (teaching module or innovation proposals). Section 3 is where you check whether their self-rating aligns with their actual task performance evidence across the pilot. Section 4 is the ultimate fluency test — can they take what they learned and make it usable for someone else?

**The one thing that matters most:** Can they teach someone else what they learned? The teaching module is the ultimate fluency test — if a colleague could follow their guide independently and succeed, the participant has internalized the competency.

**Common patterns to watch for:**
1. **Self-rating doesn't match task performance evidence.** They rate themselves "Exceeds Standard" on Diligence but scored "Meets Standard" or "Below Standard" on Task 1 classification accuracy. Or they rate themselves low on a dimension where they actually performed well. Watch for both inflation and deflation — the goal is calibration, not modesty.
2. **Teaching module is too vague for a colleague to follow independently.** The guide says things like "first, classify your data" without specifying how, or "check the output carefully" without naming what to check for. A colleague reading this without pilot training should be able to follow every step.
3. **Innovation proposals are ideas without pilot evidence grounding.** They propose "use AI for grant writing" without connecting it to anything they learned during the pilot — no governance analysis, no risk assessment, no reference to their Task 4 strategic framework. Proposals should be rooted in pilot experience, not general enthusiasm.

**What NOT to spend time on:** The narrative quality of Section 1 (pilot journey summary), whether Section 2 (strongest use case) picks the "best" use case vs. one they're passionate about, or the polish level of the teaching module. Focus on evidence alignment in the self-assessment and independence-readiness of the teaching module.

## Caseload Reality

You have 10 participants and a full CSAO workload. Here's how to manage this in ~2 hours per task cycle:

| Activity | Time | When |
|----------|------|------|
| Submission tracker check | 10 min | Days 3, 7, 10 |
| Spot-check 2 participants | 30 min | After initial assessments |
| Below Standard outreach | 15 min/person | As flagged |
| Exceeds Standard review | 10 min/person | As flagged |
| Revision spot-check | 20 min | After revisions |
| **Total per cycle** | **~2 hours** | |

**Triage rule:** If you can only review 2 participants deeply, choose: (1) the Below Standard participant most likely to disengage, and (2) the Exceeds Standard participant who could become a peer teaching resource.

---

## Quick Reference

- **Automated assessment handles:** Initial scoring, feedback delivery, revision guidance, reassessment, portfolio packaging
- **You handle:** Encouragement, calibration spot-checks, Below Standard outreach, peer exchange facilitation, escalation decisions
- **Escalation trigger:** If a participant scores Below Standard on the same dimension across two consecutive tasks, schedule a 1:1 conversation.
