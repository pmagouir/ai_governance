# Facilitator Protocol: The Workflow Designer

**Task:** pilot-task-2-create | **Window:** Weeks 3-4 (April 20 - May 1, 2026) | **Participants:** 10 across 6 units

---

## When to Review

**During the task window:**
- [ ] Day 3: Check submission tracker — who has started? Nudge anyone who hasn't opened the task.
- [ ] Day 7: Mid-window check — review any completed initial assessments. Flag anyone stuck at Below Standard for personal outreach.
- [ ] Day 10: Final check — anyone who hasn't submitted initial draft needs direct contact.

**After automated assessment runs:**
- [ ] Spot-check 2 participants (20% sample) — compare your assessment to the skill's automated assessment. Note any disagreements for calibration.
- [ ] Review all Below Standard flags — these participants may need facilitator encouragement alongside the automated reteaching module.
- [ ] Review all Exceeds Standard — confirm the extension challenge is appropriate and the participant isn't coasting.

---

## What to Prioritize

**Read first:** The Plan-vs-Reality comparison (Deliverable 3). This is the highest-signal section because it shows metacognitive growth — whether the participant can analyze the gap between what they predicted and what actually happened. A strong Plan-vs-Reality comparison names specific surprises and draws lessons. A weak one says "it went pretty much as planned."

**The one thing that matters most:** Can they trace a line from governance tier to specific prompt constraint to actual output quality?

**Common patterns to watch for:**
1. **Governance classification correct but doesn't affect the prompt design.** They classify their data as Tier 2 in the Workflow Design Document but then write a prompt with no governance constraints — as if the classification was a separate exercise rather than a design input.
2. **Edit documentation says "changed tone" without explaining audience context.** The edit log shows changes were made but doesn't connect them to why — what about the audience, purpose, or governance context drove that specific edit.
3. **Plan-vs-Reality avoids naming what went wrong.** The comparison reads as a success narrative rather than an honest analysis. Watch for "I was surprised by how well it worked" with no mention of gaps, failures, or adjustments.

**What NOT to spend time on:** Whether the participant chose a "hard enough" task from their unit's work, exact formatting of the workflow design document, or the polish level of the Claude output itself. Focus on the design thinking and reflection quality, not the output.

## Caseload Reality

You have 10 participants and a full CSAO workload. Here's how to manage this in ~2 hours per task cycle:

| Activity | Time | When |
|----------|------|------|
| Submission tracker check | 10 min | Days 3, 7, 10 |
| Spot-check 2 participants | 30 min | After initial assessments |
| Below Standard outreach | 15 min/person | As flagged |
| Exceeds Standard review | 10 min/person | As flagged |
| Revision spot-check | 20 min | After revisions |
| **Total per cycle** | **~2 hours** | |

**Triage rule:** If you can only review 2 participants deeply, choose: (1) the Below Standard participant most likely to disengage, and (2) the Exceeds Standard participant who could become a peer teaching resource.

## Quick Reference

- **Automated assessment handles:** Initial scoring, feedback delivery, revision guidance, reassessment, portfolio packaging
- **You handle:** Encouragement, calibration spot-checks, Below Standard outreach, peer exchange facilitation, escalation decisions
- **Escalation trigger:** If a participant scores Below Standard on the same dimension across two consecutive tasks, schedule a 1:1 conversation.
