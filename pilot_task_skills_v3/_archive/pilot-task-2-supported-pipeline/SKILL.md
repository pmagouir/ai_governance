## STAGE 2: DRAFT

### Your Workflow: The Four Checkpoints

You'll work through four checkpoints, each corresponding to one 4D stage. At each checkpoint, you'll articulate your thinking clearly. The checkpoints are structured; your task is real.

---

### CHECKPOINT 1: DILIGENCE — Describe and Classify Your Data

**What You'll Do:**
1. Describe the task you selected — what, for whom, by when
2. Identify what data and information is involved
3. Classify the governance tier
4. Articulate the constraints that tier creates for AI use

**Guiding Questions:**

1. **What is the task?** Be specific.
   - What deliverable are you creating? (Email, report, analysis, summary, etc.)
   - Who is it for? (Internal team, external partner, funder, scholar, etc.)
   - When is it due?
   - Example: "I draft a quarterly enrollment update email to my 6 partner universities. It's due by the 15th of the quarter-end month."

2. **What data or information is involved?**
   - List the specific data elements. Don't be vague.
   - Example: "Scholar names, enrollment status, major, GPA, financial aid type, partner institution"
   - Ask: Could any of this data identify a person? Could any of it, if combined with other data, leak identity?

3. **Apply the governance tiers.** Use the 4-tier framework:
   - **Tier 1 (Restricted):** Individual student data (names, SSN, family info, financial details). Can this prompt include Tier 1? Usually NO — you should NOT paste individual student records into Claude.
   - **Tier 2 (Sensitive):** Aggregated data with re-identification risk (cells with N < 10), sensitive org decisions. Can this prompt include Tier 2? Only if you request aggregation and ask Claude to flag small cells.
   - **Tier 3 (Internal):** Org info without identifiers (partner names, program timelines, operational counts). Can this prompt include Tier 3? YES — appropriate for Claude.
   - **Tier 4 (Public):** Already public or cleared for public. Can this prompt include Tier 4? YES — no constraints.

4. **What is the correct tier for your task?**
   - Write 3-4 sentences explaining:
     - What tier is this?
     - What data elements put it in this tier?
     - What governance concerns (if any) does this tier create?

5. **What constraints does this tier create for how you'll use AI?**
   - If Tier 1: You will NOT paste individual data into your prompt. You'll summarize conceptually.
   - If Tier 2: You will ask for aggregation and minimum cell sizes. You'll check for small cells before output is used.
   - If Tier 3/4: Standard handling; appropriate for Claude.

**Your Checkpoint 1 Deliverable:**

```
CHECKPOINT 1: DILIGENCE

Task Description:
[What: deliverable type]
[For Whom: audience]
[Timeline: when due]
[Context: why this matters]

Data Involved:
[List specific data elements]
[Note: Do any of these identify individuals? Could any be linked to identify someone?]

Governance Classification:
[Tier: 1 / 2 / 3 / 4]
[Reasoning: 3-4 sentences explaining why this tier and what it means]

Constraints for AI Use:
[Based on your tier classification, what can and can't you include in your prompt?]
[Specific handling rules for this tier in your scenario]
```

### CHECKPOINT 2: DESCRIPTION — Write Your CTCC Prompt

**What You'll Do:**
1. Write a complete CTCC prompt for your task
2. Before submitting, verify: Does your prompt respect the governance constraints from Checkpoint 1?
3. Check: Have you excluded data that shouldn't be included? Does your prompt ask for the right level of aggregation?

**Guiding Questions:**

1. **Context:** What background should Claude have?
   - What's the problem you're solving?
   - Who's the audience?
   - What's the use?
   - Example: "I'm creating a quarterly enrollment update for our 6 university partners. They want to know how many of our scholars are enrolled at their institution, what they're majoring in, and if we see any trends or concerns."

2. **Task:** What should Claude do? Be specific.
   - Not: "Help me write an email"
   - Yes: "Draft a professional, warm quarterly email to [Partner] summarizing enrollment and asking if they see patterns we should know about"

3. **Constraints:** What bounds apply?
   - Governance (from Checkpoint 1): What data should NOT be included? What aggregation level is appropriate?
   - Tone: Professional? Warm? Brief? Detailed?
   - Format: Email? Bullet points? Narrative?
   - Length: 200 words? 500?
   - Example: "Governance: no individual student data; aggregate major/enrollment only. Tone: professional but warm, like a partnership check-in. Format: email, ~300 words."

4. **Content:** What information should Claude use?
   - Specific data (partner name, enrollment numbers, majors)
   - Examples or templates (if you want a specific structure)
   - Context only you know (relationship dynamics, specific institutional needs)
   - Important: If you're giving Claude data (names, numbers, etc.), have you checked it against Checkpoint 1? Is it appropriate to include?

**Governance Compliance Self-Check:**

Before you finalize your prompt, ask:
- [ ] I classified this data as Tier __ in Checkpoint 1
- [ ] For Tier 1: I am NOT including individual student records in my prompt. ✓
- [ ] For Tier 2: I am asking Claude to aggregate to N ≥ 10 and flag small cells. ✓
- [ ] For Tier 3/4: I am comfortable including this data in the prompt. ✓
- [ ] My prompt reflects the governance tier and handling it requires.

**Your Checkpoint 2 Deliverable:**

```
CHECKPOINT 2: DESCRIPTION

CTCC Prompt:

[CONTEXT]
[Background, audience, use]

[TASK]
[Specific action Claude should take]

[CONSTRAINTS]
[Governance (from Checkpoint 1), tone, format, length, exclusions]

[CONTENT]
[Specific data, examples, context]

Governance Compliance Self-Check:
[ ] Classification from Checkpoint 1: Tier __
[ ] Data handling verified against this tier
[ ] Prompt respects governance constraints
[ ] No inappropriate data included
[ ] Aggregation level appropriate

Notes:
[Anything you're uncertain about? Any governance red flags?]
```

### CHECKPOINT 3: DELEGATION — Choose Your AI Approach

**What You'll Do:**
1. Decide: Are you handing this off to Claude, drafting together with Claude, or figuring it out together through iteration?
2. Explain your choice with reasoning that demonstrates understanding of the human-AI balance
3. Name the specific judgment, context, or verification only you can provide

**Guiding Questions:**

1. **Are you handing this off to Claude or collaborating with Claude on this task?**
   - **Hand it off (Full automation):** Claude handles the work end-to-end. You verify the output and catch any errors. Use this when the task is straightforward, inputs are clear, and you can easily spot mistakes. Example: Claude generates a formatted data summary from a dataset you provide.
   - **Draft together (Collaboration):** Claude drafts or produces initial work; you refine it with your judgment, context, or relationship knowledge. Use this when the work benefits from a starting point, but your human perspective is essential to make it right. Example: Claude drafts an email, you add relationship-specific context about tone preferences.
   - **Figure it out together (Iteration):** You and Claude work back-and-forth, refining the approach as you go. Use this when the task requires ongoing problem-solving, experimentation, or when the direction isn't fully clear upfront. Example: You and Claude collaborate to redesign a process or troubleshoot a complex problem.

2. **What specific judgment, context, or verification do you bring that Claude can't?**
   - Don't say "oversight" or "human judgment" (too vague). Be specific.
   - Good: "Relationship knowledge of each partner's communication style" or "Verification of enrollment numbers against our database" or "Understanding of which questions resonate with funders"
   - Good: "Strategic judgment about which data points matter to my audience" or "Knowledge of what's happened since last quarter that changes the narrative"

3. **Why does this balance fit YOUR task?**
   - Explain how your governance classification (Checkpoint 1) and prompt design (Checkpoint 2) point toward this approach.
   - Example: "I'm drafting together because my Tier 2 classification means I need to verify aggregation before sending. Claude can create the structure and numbers, but I must check that no cell is smaller than 10 and that the story makes sense for this quarter."

**Your Checkpoint 3 Deliverable:**

```

AI Approach:
[Hand it off / Draft together / Figure it out together]

Reasoning:
- What Claude does well in this approach: [specific]
- What you bring to this task: [specific judgment/context/verification that only you can provide]
- How your governance classification (Checkpoint 1) and prompt (Checkpoint 2) align with this approach: [explanation]

Human-AI Balance in Practice:
[Specific statement of what only you will provide in this interaction, and why Claude can't do it]
```

### CHECKPOINT 4: DISCERNMENT — Run, Audit, Edit, Judge

**What You'll Do:**
1. Run your CTCC prompt in Claude
2. Audit the output critically
3. Document every edit you make with a tag and rationale
4. Write your human judgment layer: what did you verify or check?
5. Retrospective: what would you prompt differently next time?

**Step 4a: Run Your Prompt**

Copy your CTCC prompt from Checkpoint 2 into Claude and run it. Paste Claude's full output into your deliverable.

**Step 4b: Audit the Output**

Read Claude's output with a critical eye. Ask:
- **Task completion:** Does it do what you asked? (If you asked for an email, did you get an email? If you asked for a summary, is it summarized well?)
- **Accuracy:** Are facts correct? Are names spelled right? Are numbers accurate (if you included data)?
- **Tone:** Does it sound right for your audience and your relationship with them?
- **Governance:** For your specific task, does it expose data that shouldn't be exposed? Does it handle the tier appropriately?
- **Completeness:** Is anything missing? Did Claude make assumptions you need to check?
- **Useability:** Would you send this to your audience as-is, or does it need work?

Write 4-5 sentences describing:
- What Claude did well (be specific)
- What needs to be better or different (be specific)

**Step 4c: Document Your Edits**

Make the improvements you identified. For EVERY edit, use this format:

```
[SECTION or PARAGRAPH]
Original: [exact text from Claude's output]
Revised: [your revised text]
Tag: [Accuracy Fix / Tone Adjustment / Strategic Addition / Removal]
Rationale: [Specific explanation of why this change was necessary for YOUR context. Reference your audience, governance concern, accuracy, relationship context, or other specific factor.]
```

Include at least 2-3 edits per checkpoint (often you'll have more).

**Example — Good Edit Documentation:**
```
[Closing paragraph]
Original: "Please let us know if you have questions or if our scholars need additional support."
Revised: "I'd love to hear your thoughts — are you seeing any trends from your end? And let me know if there's anything we can do to better support your team in advising our scholars."
Tag: Tone Adjustment
Rationale: The original is formal and one-directional. My partners expect a conversational, collaborative tone; they appreciate when I ask for their input, not just when I report data. This revision invites dialogue and shows I'm interested in their perspective, not just making a statement.
```

**Step 4d: Human Judgment Layer**

Before this output goes to your audience, what do you check or verify? Write 5-6 sentences addressing:

For any task:
- **Accuracy:** What would you spot-check or verify against known facts?
- **Governance:** What would you confirm is handled correctly?
- **Context:** What do you know about your audience that Claude doesn't? How does that shape your judgment?

For your specific task, add:
- **Relationship or tone verification:** If this is going to a partner or colleague, what relationship dynamics matter?
- **Strategic judgment:** What bigger-picture thinking does this output need that Claude can't provide?
- **Risk mitigation:** What could go wrong, and how are you mitigating it?

Example:
"Before this email goes to the deans, I'd verify: (1) the enrollment numbers match my database — I'll spot-check Trinity's count against our records. (2) The tone fits my relationship with each partner — Trinity is a smooth partner who appreciates brevity; I'll skim to confirm my additions didn't make this too long. (3) I know Howard's dean is concerned about retention; my revisions added a note about our retention support, which matters to her. (4) Governance: I've confirmed we're only reporting aggregate enrollment, not individual student names or details. (5) I'd consider whether to personalize the opening for each dean or send a generic version — the output is generic, but I might add a quick line for each partner when I send it."

**Step 4e: Retrospective — Prompt Improvement**

Now that you've audited and edited Claude's output, what did you learn about your prompt? If you were to run this task again next quarter, what would you change about your CTCC prompt to reduce the edit burden?

Write 2-3 sentences identifying:
- What edit pattern did you notice? (Did you keep adding tone? Keep fixing the same kind of error?)
- What would you change in your CTCC prompt to prevent this edit in the future?
- What would you ask Claude to do differently?

Example:
"I notice I added relationship context to every partner's section — Tony at Trinity, the enrollment concern at Howard, the retention focus at GW. My prompt was generic to all partners; if I knew I'd need personalization, I should have asked Claude to create individual versions for each partner, or asked for a template that I could customize. Next time, I'll ask: 'Draft this email customized for [Partner], considering [specific context I know]' rather than writing a generic version I then customize manually."

## STAGE 3: ASSESS

### Assessment Framework

Your work will be assessed on FOUR dimensions. Each has four qualitative levels: **Exceeds Standard, Meets Standard, Approaching Standard, Below Standard.**

Note: This task includes a NEW dimension — **Pipeline Coherence** — that measures whether the four stages tell a consistent story.

---

### Dimension 1: Governance Integration

**What This Measures**
You correctly classify the governance tier for your real scenario. Your tier classification is consistent with the data you described. Your CTCC prompt respects the governance constraints of that tier (no Tier 1 data pasted, appropriate aggregation for Tier 2, safe handling for Tier 3/4). Your audit addresses governance concerns specific to your scenario.

**Why It Matters**
In Task 1, governance was scaffolded (you had an answer key). In Task 2, it's not. You're classifying real data you handle every day. Mistakes here affect the actual security of data in your unit. Getting this right means knowing what you can and can't ask Claude to process with your data.

**Exceeds Standard**
- Tier classification is correct and well-reasoned. Reasoning references specific data elements and the governance framework.
- Constraints from that tier are explicitly stated and specific to your scenario (not generic). Example: "Tier 2 aggregation with N ≥ 10 threshold" rather than "data sensitivity."
- CTCC prompt demonstrably reflects governance constraints. (E.g., if Tier 2, the prompt asks for aggregation; if Tier 1 elements are involved, they're handled conceptually, not pasted raw.)
- Audit findings specifically address governance concerns for your task. If there were re-identification risks or data handling questions, you identified them and addressed them in your edits.
- Governance verification is explicit in human judgment layer: you name specific checks you'd make.

**Meets Standard**
- Tier classification is correct with reasonable reasoning.
- Constraints are stated and reasonably specific to your scenario.
- CTCC prompt respects governance constraints. You avoid including inappropriate data; you ask for aggregation where needed.
- Your audit touches on governance considerations.
- Human judgment layer mentions governance verification.

**Approaching Standard**
- Tier classification is correct, but reasoning is vague or incomplete.
- Constraints are mentioned but not fully operationalized. (E.g., "Be careful with sensitive data" rather than "Request N ≥ 10 aggregation.")
- CTCC prompt generally respects constraints, but there may be a mismatch. (E.g., you classified as Tier 2 but your prompt includes data that needs aggregation without asking for it.)

### Dimension 2: Prompt + Human-AI Balance

**What This Measures**
Your CTCC prompt includes all four elements (Context, Task, Constraints, Content) and is specific to your actual scenario. Your choice of AI approach (hand off, draft together, or figure it out together) is defensible and based on reasoning that demonstrates understanding of the human-AI balance.

**Why It Matters**
A well-designed prompt produces better output, which means less editing and faster work. Choosing the right AI approach ensures you're using Claude the way it's designed — not forcing Claude to do something that needs human judgment, and not wasting human time on something Claude can handle.

**Exceeds Standard**

- Prompt is highly specific to YOUR scenario, not generic. (Names, details, context unique to your task and audience are included; assumptions Claude might make are addressed.)

**Meets Standard**

You identify real errors or improvements in Claude's output. Every edit is documented with the correct tag and a specific, context-aware rationale. Your rationales reference your specific audience, governance concern, or scenario context — not generic explanations.

**Why It Matters**

### Dimension 4: Pipeline Coherence (NEW)

**What This Measures**
The four checkpoints tell a consistent story: governance classification shapes your prompt, your prompt informs your choice of AI approach (hand off, draft together, or figure it out together), your audit reveals whether the prompt was effective, and your retrospective connects edit patterns back to prompt improvements. Everything connects logically.

**Why It Matters**
In Task 1, you learned each stage. In Task 2, you're supposed to prove you understand how they work together. Someone who can execute each stage in isolation is different from someone who understands the system. This dimension measures the system-level understanding.

**Exceeds Standard**
- Governance tier (Checkpoint 1) visibly shapes CTCC prompt (Checkpoint 2). Evidence: If you classified Tier 2, your prompt asks for aggregation. If you identified a governance concern, your prompt addresses it.
- AI approach choice (Checkpoint 3) aligns with prompt and governance. Reasoning demonstrates understanding of how the governance tier and prompt design together indicate the right human-AI balance.
- Audit findings (Checkpoint 4) reference back to governance and prompt design. If you identified an edit, you can explain why that pattern suggests a prompt improvement.
- Retrospective explicitly connects edit patterns to prompt improvements. (E.g., "I kept adding relationship context manually; next time I'll ask Claude to customize for each partner." This shows you understand the feedback loop between audit and prompt.)
- Overall narrative: You can articulate how this four-stage process works and how each stage informs the next.

**Meets Standard**
- Governance and prompt show alignment. Prompt reflects the tier you classified.
- AI approach choice is reasonable given your scenario and governance constraints.
- Audit addresses real issues in Claude's output.
- Retrospective connects some edits back to potential prompt improvements.
- The four stages generally tell a coherent story, though connections may not be as explicit as Exceeds.

**Approaching Standard**
- Governance and prompt are somewhat aligned, but connections aren't explicit. (You classified correctly and wrote a good prompt, but you don't articulate how the classification shaped the prompt.)
- AI approach choice is reasonable but may not deeply reflect governance or prompt specifics.
- Audit finds edits but doesn't connect them to prompt design.
- Retrospective may be vague: "Next time I'd ask for better tone" rather than "My prompt didn't specify tone preferences for different audiences, so I'll request customization next time."
- The four stages feel somewhat disconnected.

**Below Standard**
- Governance and prompt don't clearly align. (You classified as Tier 2 but your prompt doesn't address aggregation.)
- AI approach choice doesn't reference governance or prompt concerns.
- Audit finds edits but doesn't reflect on why they were necessary or what they reveal.
- Retrospective is missing or doesn't address prompt improvement.
- The four stages feel like separate exercises rather than a coherent system.

**Calibration Anchors (Approaching → Meets Boundary)**

My edits (Checkpoint 4) revealed that Claude didn't flag small cells, so next time (Retrospective) I'll ask for specific handling of edge cases."
